Beaver, Pennsylvania

Location

Beaver Pennsylvania is a small town located northwest of the city of Pittsburgh. The town is nested in the confluence between the Beaver river and the Ohio river. Beaver is only about 30mins from the Ohio border and is in line with the City of Erie from a longitudinal stand point.

About

Weather

In Beaver, the summers are warm and partly cloudy and the winters are freezing, snowy, and mostly cloudy. Over the course of the year, the temperature typically varies from 21°F to 83°F and is rarely below 5°F or above 90°F. Based on the tourism score, the best time of year to visit Beaver for warm-weather activities is from mid June to mid September.

Commerce

Commerce in the town of Beaver largely consists of independently owned and managed small businesses. The community supports local artisans, restaurants, and tradesmen. Beaver is the county seat of Beaver County, and stands as a dry town which creates an interesting dynamic for restaurants. Due to the presence of the county hospital and courthouse, the 3 block main street of the town is bustling with activity and clientele looking to shop and eat.

Events

The town of Beaver is home to a number of unique events and festivals. In the Spring, the Brady’s Run Maple Syrup festival brings tourists for war re-enactments, a fair, & a mile long line for the local maple syrup doused pancakes and sausage breakfast. Summertime brings the Beaver Car Cruise, the Beaver County Boom Independence Day firework festival, & a craft vendor fair called Garrison Days, as well as a number of local food truck events. In Autumn, Beaver hosts multiple parades and a town-wide trick-or-treating event. For the winter season, Light Up Night draws a crowd with local shopping deals, restaurant specials, & a fireworks display. In addition to all of this, there is an organization called Underground Beaver that plans monthly special events with ticket sales, such as a Shamrock pub crawl, scavenger hunts, and an annual Luau.
